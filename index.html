Repeat*3 is an experimental typographic system that treats letterforms not as carriers of language, but as evolving visual structures.

Through repetition, rotation, symmetry, and layered interpolation, characters are continuously generated and transformed into dynamic, multi-layered compositions.

Built within a code-driven framework, letters and numbers gradually move beyond legibility, becoming abstract forms defined by rhythm, spatial depth, and structural tension.

Rather than producing a stable typeface, Repeat*3 operates as an open field for exploring expressive, visually driven typography.

It is particularly suited to experimental layouts, visual identities, digital media, and typographic compositions where form and structure take precedence over readability.
